# City Stripes Beanie

A bottom-up ribbed beanie with clean color blocks and a removable pom-pom.

## Sizes
| Size | Head circumference | Finished circumference | Finished height |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Toddler | 18-19 in | 16 in | 7.5 in |
| Child | 20-21 in | 18 in | 8.5 in |
| Adult S/M | 21-23 in | 19.5 in | 9.25 in |
| Adult L/XL | 23-25 in | 21.5 in | 10 in |

## Materials
Worsted-weight wool or acrylic:
- MC mustard: 70 (85, 100, 120) yd
- A charcoal: 45 (55, 65, 75) yd
- B cream: 35 (45, 55, 65) yd
- C blush: 30 (35, 40, 45) yd
- 4 mm 16 in circular needle for brim
- 4.5 mm 16 in circular and DPNs or magic-loop needle
- Marker, needle, pom-pom maker
- Optional snap for removable pom

## Gauge
18 sts and 24 rounds = 4 in in stockinette on larger needles after blocking.

## Cast on
Using smaller needle and German twisted cast-on, CO:
72 (80, 88, 96) sts.
Join without twisting and place marker.

## Brim
Work *k2, p2* rib for:
2 (2.25, 2.5, 2.75) in.

Switch to larger needle.

## Body stripe sequence
Knit all rounds.
- MC: 8 rounds
- A: 7 rounds
- B: 5 rounds
- C: 6 rounds
- A: 7 rounds
- B: 5 rounds
- MC: continue until work measures 6 (7, 7.75, 8.5) in from cast-on edge.

Carry colors loosely up inside when the gap is fewer than 8 rounds; otherwise cut and weave later.

## Crown setup
The stitch count is divisible by 8. Place a marker every 9 (10, 11, 12) stitches.

### Decrease rounds
Round 1: *K to 2 sts before marker, k2tog; repeat from * around. 8 sts decreased.
Round 2: Knit.
Repeat Rounds 1-2 until 40 sts remain.

Then work decrease round every round until 8 sts remain.

Cut yarn leaving 10 in. Thread through remaining stitches twice, pull closed, and secure inside.

## Pom-pom
Make a 3-4 in pom using all colors. For removable construction, sew one half of a snap to the hat crown and the other to the pom ties. Otherwise thread ties through crown and knot securely inside.

## Finishing
Weave ends, avoiding tight spots at color changes. Soak, squeeze gently, shape over a bowl or balloon matching finished circumference, and dry.

## Customization
- For a folded double brim, work 5 in ribbing.
- Add slouch by knitting 1.5-2 in more before crown shaping.
- Use duplicate stitch to add initials after blocking.
- To resize, cast on a multiple of 8 based on gauge and desired 10-15 percent negative ease.

## Troubleshooting
**Jog at stripe change:** Use jogless stripes: on the second round of a new color, lift the stitch below the first stitch and knit it together with the first stitch.
**Brim loose:** Use a needle 1 mm smaller or twisted rib.
**Crown pointy:** Begin decreases 0.5 in earlier.
