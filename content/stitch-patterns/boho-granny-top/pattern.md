# Boho Granny Top

A cropped lace-up camisole with triangular cups, granny-stitch body, and adjustable shoulder and back ties.

## Fit
Designed with 0-2 in negative ease at the underbust. Cup coverage is adjustable through added rows.

## Materials
- Sport-weight cotton or cotton-linen:
  - XS/S: 650 yd
  - M/L: 800 yd
  - XL/2X: 950 yd
  - 3X/4X: 1,150 yd
  - 5X: 1,300 yd
- 3.5 mm hook
- Markers and needle
- Optional 0.5 yd lining fabric

## Gauge
22 dc and 11 rows = 4 in in granny-stitch fabric after blocking.

## Measurements
Choose by full bust, then customize cup depth.
XS 30-32, S 34-36, M 38-40, L 42-44, XL 46-48, 2X 50-52, 3X 54-56, 4X 58-60, 5X 62-64 in.

## Cup - make 2
Ch 13.

Row 1: Dc in fourth ch from hook and next 8 ch, 5 dc in last ch. Rotate to work along opposite side of foundation chain; dc in next 10 chains. Turn. 25 dc including turning chain.

Row 2: Ch 3, dc in each stitch to center of 5-dc group, 5 dc in center stitch, dc in each remaining stitch. Turn.

Repeat Row 2 until cup side edge measures:
- XS/S: 6.5 in
- M/L: 7.5 in
- XL/2X: 8.5 in
- 3X/4X: 9.5 in
- 5X: 10.5 in

For more coverage, continue rows. Fasten off.

## Cup edging
Join yarn at lower inner corner. Sc evenly up inner edge, work (sc, ch 2, sc) at cup tip, sc down outer edge and across lower edge. Fasten off.

## Lower body foundation
Lay cups side by side with 0.5-1 in between inner lower corners. Join with a chain bridge:
- XS/S: ch 2
- M/L: ch 4
- XL/2X: ch 6
- 3X/4X: ch 8
- 5X: ch 10

With RS facing, sc across bottom of first cup, work sc across bridge, sc across second cup. Do not cut yarn.

At each outer cup edge, chain enough to reach around the back with a 3-5 in center opening. Total foundation should fit the underbust with 0-2 in negative ease and contain a multiple of 3 stitches plus 2. Join carefully if making a pullover version; otherwise turn rows for a lace-up back.

## Body
Row 1: Ch 3, 2 dc in first stitch, *skip 2, 3 dc in next; repeat across, ending with dc in last stitch. Turn.
Row 2: Ch 3, work 3 dc in each space between groups across, dc in turning chain. Turn.
Repeat Row 2 until body measures 7-11 in below cups, or desired length.

## Hem
Row 1: Ch 1, sc across.
Row 2: Ch 1, *sc, skip 2, 5 dc in next, skip 2; repeat, ending with sc. Adjust spacing slightly at ends.
Fasten off.

## Back lacing edge
Along each open back edge work:
Row 1: Sc evenly.
Row 2: Ch 1, *sc in next 3, ch 3, skip approximately 2 row ends; repeat to create loops.
Fasten off.

Make a cord approximately 60-80 in:
Ch desired length, sl st back through every chain. Lace through back loops from bottom to top.

## Shoulder ties - make 2
Join at cup tip. Ch 70-100 depending on desired tie length. Sl st back along chain. Fasten off.

For wider non-tie straps:
Join at tip and work 5 sc rows until strap reaches shoulder. Sew to upper back edge.

## Optional lining
Trace each blocked cup onto cotton jersey or lightweight woven fabric, adding 0.5 in seam allowance. Turn edges under and hand sew to the wrong side, leaving the outer crochet edge free.

## Finishing
Wet block to measurements. Shape cup points without overstretching. Weave in ends.

## Fit checks
- Cup should cover without the outer edge cutting across breast tissue.
- Underbust band should be supportive but allow two fingers underneath.
- Add cup rows before widening the body.
- Add a row of elastic thread inside the underbust edge for extra support.

## Troubleshooting
**Cup gapes:** Use a smaller hook for final cup rows or add elastic along top edge.
**Center pulls apart:** Shorten the center bridge or add a decorative tie.
**Body flares:** Reduce one 3-dc group at each side over two rows.
