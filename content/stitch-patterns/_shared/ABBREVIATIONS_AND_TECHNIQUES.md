# Shared Abbreviations and Techniques

## Crochet - US terminology
- beg: beginning
- BLO: back loop only
- ch: chain
- dc: double crochet
- dec: decrease
- FLO: front loop only
- hdc: half double crochet
- inc: increase
- MR: magic ring
- rep: repeat
- rnd: round
- RS: right side
- sc: single crochet
- sk: skip
- sl st: slip stitch
- sp: space
- st(s): stitch(es)
- tr: treble crochet
- WS: wrong side
- yo: yarn over

### Adjustable ring
Wrap yarn around two fingers, insert hook into ring, pull up a loop, then work the first round into the ring. Pull the tail to close.

### Invisible single-crochet decrease
Insert hook through the front loop of each of the next two stitches, yarn over and pull through both front loops, yarn over and pull through the remaining two loops.

### Standing double crochet
With a slip knot on the hook, yarn over, insert into the indicated space, pull up a loop, and complete a double crochet. This produces a cleaner color-change join than a chain-3.

### Mattress seam for crochet
Place pieces side by side. Working from the right side, pass the needle under corresponding edge bars, alternating from one piece to the other. Pull gently every few inches.

## Knitting
- BO: bind off
- CO: cast on
- DPN: double-pointed needle
- k: knit
- k2tog: knit two together
- M1L: make one left
- M1R: make one right
- p: purl
- pm: place marker
- rep: repeat
- rnd: round
- RS: right side
- ssk: slip, slip, knit
- st(s): stitch(es)
- WS: wrong side
- yo: yarn over

### Long-tail cast-on
Use a yarn tail approximately three times the cast-on edge. Form stitches over the needle using the thumb and index finger sling-shot method.

### German twisted cast-on
A stretchier alternative to long-tail cast-on; useful for hat brims and sweater hems.

### Mattress stitch for knitting
With right sides facing up, sew under the horizontal bar between the first and second stitch on alternating sides.

## Embroidery
- 2 strands means two separated strands from standard six-strand embroidery floss.
- Backstitch: solid outline stitch.
- French knot: wrapped knot for flower centers and texture.
- Lazy daisy: detached chain used as a petal or leaf.
- Long-and-short stitch: blended fill.
- Satin stitch: parallel fill stitches.
- Split stitch: outline stitch with a softly textured line.
- Stem stitch: slightly twisted line ideal for stems.

## Gauge
Gauge is the number of stitches and rows in a measured area after washing or blocking. Always make a swatch larger than the measurement area. If you have too many stitches, use a larger tool. If you have too few, use a smaller tool.
