# Sunday Grid Cardigan

A cropped button cardigan with a clean patchwork grid, ribbed bands, and softly dropped shoulders.

## Construction
Four large rectangles form the body and sleeves. Surface slip stitch is added after assembly to create the pastel grid.

## Sizing
Designed for 6-10 in positive ease.

Finished chest:
38 (42, 46, 50, 54, 58, 62, 66, 70) in.
Finished length:
19 (20, 21, 22, 23, 24, 25, 26, 27) in.

## Materials
DK yarn:
- MC cream: 1,050 (1,150, 1,250, 1,400, 1,550, 1,700, 1,850, 2,050, 2,250) yd
- Contrast colors total: 350-750 yd depending on size
4 mm hook, 3.5 mm hook for ribbing, 5 buttons.

## Gauge
18 hdc and 14 rows = 4 in after blocking.

## Back
With 4 mm hook, ch:
88 (96, 106, 114, 124, 132, 142, 150, 160).

Row 1: Hdc in second ch and across.
Continue hdc rows to target length less 2 in hem band:
17 (18, 19, 20, 21, 22, 23, 24, 25) in.
Fasten off.

## Fronts - make 2
Chain enough for half back width minus 1 in for center bands:
40 (44, 49, 53, 58, 62, 67, 71, 76).
Work hdc to same length as back.

For V-neck option, begin decreasing one stitch at neck edge every fourth row during final 8-10 in. For the pictured crew-neck version, leave rectangular.

## Sleeves - make 2
Ch 58 (60, 64, 66, 70, 74, 78, 82, 86).
Work hdc for 17-19 in depending on arm length.
For taper, decrease one stitch at each edge every 10th row four times before cuff.

## Grid plan
Block panels first. Using markers, divide each panel into approximately 4 in squares:
- vertical lines every 18 stitches
- horizontal lines every 14 rows

With contrast yarn and RS facing, surface slip stitch along marked vertical and horizontal lines. Keep loops loose so fabric does not pucker. Alternate pink, sage, lavender, blue, and oatmeal lines. Do not carry yarn across the wrong side; fasten each line separately.

## Assembly
Seam shoulders, leaving 8-12 in neck opening by size.
Center sleeves over shoulder seams and attach.
Seam sleeves and sides.

## Hem ribbing
With smaller hook, ch 11.
Row 1: Sc in second ch and across. Sl st in next 2 body-edge stitches. Turn.
Row 2: Skip 2 sl sts, sc BLO across ribbing. Turn.
Row 3: Ch 1, sc BLO across, sl st into next 2 body stitches. Turn.
Repeat around lower edge. Seam ribbing ends.

## Cuffs
Repeat attached ribbing using ch 9.

## Front and neck band
Using smaller hook, work attached ribbing continuously from lower right front, around neck, and down left front using ch 8.

Place five buttonholes on wearer's right side by replacing two center ribbing stitches with ch 2 in one row. On return row, work 2 sc into chain space.

Sew buttons opposite holes.

## Finishing
Weave every surface-stitch tail securely. Steam or wet block lightly, avoiding stretching grid lines.

## Customization
- Use a single contrast color for a minimalist windowpane effect.
- Lengthen body before adding grid and ribbing.
- Add pockets before surface grid so lines can continue across pocket fronts.
- For a pullover, omit center opening and work one full front.

## Troubleshooting
**Grid puckers:** Redo surface slip stitches with a larger hook and looser loops.
**Panels skew:** Hdc turning chains must be consistent; block before decorating.
**Band flares:** Attach to fewer edge points.
