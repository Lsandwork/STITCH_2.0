# Yarn Substitution and Care

Choose a substitute by matching all four factors:
1. Yarn weight
2. Gauge
3. Fiber behavior
4. Meterage, not only skein count

Cotton is structured and can become heavy. Wool is elastic and blocks well. Acrylic is washable and economical but may grow with heat. Alpaca drapes and stretches. Chenille is plush but hides stitches and can shed.

Buy 10 percent extra for solid-color projects and 15 percent extra for multi-color projects. Keep ball bands and alternate skeins when dye lots differ.

Wash the gauge swatch exactly as the finished piece will be washed. Record pre- and post-wash measurements.

General care:
- Cotton accessories: cool gentle wash, reshape, dry flat.
- Wool garments: hand wash or wool cycle, no agitation, dry flat.
- Acrylic: cool wash, low heat or dry flat.
- Embroidery: hand wash only if all fibers are colorfast; press face-down over a towel.
- Stuffed toys: spot clean unless every component is machine washable.
