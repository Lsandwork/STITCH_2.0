# Sunset Market Bag

A structured cotton tote made from raised-flower granny squares, a solid base, and reinforced crocheted handles.

## Finished measurements
Approximately 15 in wide x 14 in tall x 4 in deep. Handles add approximately 11 in of drop.

## Materials
- Worsted-weight mercerized cotton:
  - MC warm cream: 550 yd
  - A coral: 90 yd
  - B golden yellow: 70 yd
  - C dusty rose: 70 yd
  - D sage: 100 yd
  - E terracotta: 80 yd
- 4.5 mm crochet hook, or size needed for gauge
- Tapestry needle
- Locking markers
- Optional: 16 x 15 in cotton lining, magnetic snap, plastic canvas for base

## Gauge
One blocked flower square after Round 5 measures 4.75 in. Gauge is more important than hook size.

## Abbreviations
US crochet terms. See shared glossary.

## Special stitches
**Beginning cluster:** Ch 2, work 2 dc in the same stitch, leaving the last loop of each dc on hook; yarn over and pull through all loops.

**3-dc cluster:** Work 3 dc in the indicated stitch, leaving the last loop of each dc on hook; yarn over and pull through all loops.

**Picot:** Ch 3, sl st in the first chain.

## Make 18 flower squares

### Round 1 - flower center
With A, make MR. Ch 1, work 12 sc into ring. Join with sl st to first sc. Fasten off. 12 sc.

### Round 2 - petals
Join B in any stitch. Ch 2, 2 dc in same stitch, ch 2, sl st in same stitch. *Sl st in next stitch, ch 2, 2 dc in same stitch, ch 2, sl st in same stitch; repeat from * around. Join. Fasten off. 12 petals.

### Round 3 - leaf foundation
Turn the flower slightly so you can work behind the petals. Join D around the post between any two petals. Ch 1, sc around same post, ch 3. *Sc around next post, ch 3; repeat around. Join to first sc. 12 ch-3 spaces.

### Round 4 - square shaping
Sl st into first ch-3 space. Ch 3, 2 dc, ch 2, 3 dc in same space for first corner. In next two spaces work 3 dc in each. *(3 dc, ch 2, 3 dc) in next space, 3 dc in each of next two spaces; repeat from * twice. Join. Fasten off.

### Round 5 - final square
Join MC in any corner. Ch 3, 2 dc, ch 2, 3 dc in same corner. Work 3 dc in each space across to next corner. *(3 dc, ch 2, 3 dc) in corner; work 3 dc in each space to next corner; repeat from * around. Join and fasten off.

Make six squares using A/B centers, four using C/A, four using E/B, and four using B/C. Block every square to 4.75 in.

## Base panel
With MC, ch 59.

Row 1: Sc in second ch from hook and across. Turn. 58 sc.
Rows 2-15: Ch 1, sc across. Turn.
The panel should measure approximately 14 x 4 in. Adjust rows only if needed to match the width of three joined squares.

## Layout
Front and back each use a 3-square-wide by 3-square-high grid. Arrange colors before joining.

## Join squares
Use MC and mattress seam or slip-stitch join. Join horizontal rows first, then vertical columns. Keep seams flexible and consistent.

## Assemble bag
1. Place front and back wrong sides together.
2. Center one long edge of base panel along the bottom of front. Seam.
3. Seam the opposite long base edge to the back.
4. Fold base short ends upward to form the side depth.
5. Seam side edges from upper corner down, around base end, and up the opposite panel. Repeat second side.
6. Turn right side out.

## Upper band
Join MC at a side seam.

Round 1: Ch 1. Work sc evenly around the opening, placing one sc in each dc and approximately two sc per three row ends at seams. Adjust to an even total divisible by 4. Join.
Rounds 2-5: Ch 1, sc in each stitch around. Join.
Round 6: Reverse sc around for a corded finish. Fasten off.

## Handles - make 2
With MC, ch 76.

Row 1: Sc in second chain and across. 75 sc.
Rows 2-5: Ch 1, sc BLO across. Turn.
Fasten off leaving a 20 in tail at each end.

Mark handle placement 3.5 in from each side seam. Overlap each handle end 2.5 in inside the bag. Sew a rectangle with an X through the center using doubled yarn. Repeat all four attachment points.

## Optional lining
Cut two rectangles 16 x 15.5 in and one 15 x 5 in base. Add 0.5 in seam allowance. Sew lining, press upper edge under, insert into bag, and hand sew below the upper band. Add magnetic snap before installation.

## Finishing
Weave in all ends. Wet block lightly without flattening petals. Allow to dry completely.

## Customization
- Add two more squares to each face for a larger tote.
- Replace flower centers with solid granny squares for a quieter design.
- For a firmer bag, use cotton held with a strand of lace-weight nylon.
- Do not lengthen handles without reinforcement; cotton stretches under load.

## Troubleshooting
**Squares curl:** Block before joining and reduce tension in the petal round.
**Bag twists:** Check that every square faces the same direction and seams have equal tension.
**Handles stretch:** Add woven webbing or a fabric strip behind each handle.
