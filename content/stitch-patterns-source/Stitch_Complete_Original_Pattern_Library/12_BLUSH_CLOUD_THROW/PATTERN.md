# Blush Cloud Throw

A plush pink blanket combining garter borders with an oversized broken-basket texture.

## Materials
Super-bulky washable yarn:
- Crib: 900 yd
- Throw: 1,550 yd
- Large: 2,250 yd
12 mm circular needle at least 36 in long.
Needle for ends.

## Gauge
7 sts and 10 rows = 4 in in pattern after blocking.

## Stitch multiple
Main pattern is a multiple of 8 plus 4. Add 10 border stitches, five at each side.

Cast on:
- Crib: 64 sts
- Throw: 88 sts
- Large: 104 sts

These counts include borders.

## Bottom border
Knit 10 rows.

## Main pattern
Keep first and last 5 stitches knit on every row.

Rows 1-4: K5, *k4, p4; repeat from * to last 9 sts, k4, k5.
Rows 5-8: K5, *p4, k4; repeat from * to last 9 sts, p4, k5.
Rows 9-12: Knit all stitches.
Rows 13-16: K5, *p4, k4; repeat from * to last 9 sts, p4, k5.
Rows 17-20: K5, *k4, p4; repeat from * to last 9 sts, k4, k5.
Rows 21-24: Knit all stitches.

Repeat Rows 1-24 until piece measures:
- Crib: 40 in
- Throw: 58 in
- Large: 70 in
from cast-on, ending after Row 12 or 24.

## Top border
Knit 10 rows. Bind off loosely in knit.

## Optional tassels
Cut yarn into 14 in lengths. Use five strands per tassel. Attach one tassel at each corner and additional tassels every 6-8 in along short ends. Tighten knots firmly.

## Finishing
Weave ends by splitting plies where possible. Wash on gentle cycle only if yarn label permits. Support entire wet blanket and dry flat.

## Custom sizing
At this gauge, each added 8-stitch repeat adds approximately 4.5 in. Add length by completing more 24-row repeats.

## Troubleshooting
**Edges pull inward:** Keep side garter stitches relaxed or use a needle one size larger for border stitches.
**Blanket is too heavy:** Substitute a lighter chainette-style super bulky yarn.
**Texture looks flat:** Block gently; do not press.
