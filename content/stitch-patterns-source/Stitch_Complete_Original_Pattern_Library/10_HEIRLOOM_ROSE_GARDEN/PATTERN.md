# Heirloom Rose Garden Embroidery

A layered central rose surrounded by small flowers, buds, and curved foliage.

## Materials
- 10 x 10 in linen
- 7 in hoop
- Floss in 12 colors: three rose shades, two pinks, cream, yellow, three greens, blue, brown
- Needles 5-9
- Transfer method
- Optional lightweight woven fusible stabilizer

## Preparation
Fuse stabilizer to wrong side only if fabric is loose. Transfer design. Keep at least 1 in between outer leaves and hoop edge.

## Stitch map
- Rose: long-and-short stitch, 2 strands
- Rose outline accents: split stitch, 1 strand
- Leaves: fishbone stitch, 2 strands
- Stems: stem stitch, 2 strands
- Small flowers: lazy daisy, 2-3 strands
- Centers: French knots, 2 strands
- Buds: padded satin stitch, 2 strands

## Central rose

### Foundation
Outline the rose with 1 strand darkest rose using split stitch. Mark petal overlaps clearly.

### Center spiral
Using darkest rose and 2 strands, work short curved satin stitches around the center. Keep stitches under 1/8 in.

### Inner petals
Work long-and-short stitch from petal edge toward center using darkest and middle rose. Place first row of stitches unevenly to avoid a striped edge. Blend by bringing the lighter shade up between darker stitches.

### Outer petals
Use middle and light rose. Follow petal direction: stitches should fan outward and curve around the flower. Add a few dark split-stitch shadows only where petals overlap.

## Secondary flowers
Work five-petal lazy daisies in pink and cream. Use 3 strands for larger blooms and 2 for smaller. Add yellow two-wrap French knots.

## Blue accent flowers
Create five straight stitches from each center. Add a blue French knot at center.

## Buds
Lay one small horizontal padding stitch. Cover with 6-10 vertical satin stitches. Add a green split-stitch calyx at the base.

## Leaves
Work center vein first. Fill with fishbone stitch, alternating two green shades. Keep tips sharp by ending the first stitch precisely at the leaf point.

## Stems and vines
Work main curves in stem stitch. Use one strand for the finest tendrils.

## Final detail pass
Add:
- dark green vein accents
- three cream seed knots
- tiny brown straight-stitch thorns on two stems
- one or two pink highlight stitches on the rose

## Finishing
Remove transfer marks. Press from wrong side over a thick towel. Mount in hoop. Gather rear fabric and cover with felt.

## Troubleshooting
**Rose looks flat:** Increase value contrast at petal overlaps rather than adding more outline.
**Long-and-short looks striped:** Vary stitch lengths and split previous stitches.
**Fabric puckers:** Shorten stitches and release hoop tension between sessions.
