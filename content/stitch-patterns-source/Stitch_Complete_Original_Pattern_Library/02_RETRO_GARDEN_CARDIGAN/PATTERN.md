# Retro Garden Cardigan

A relaxed drop-shoulder cardigan built from floral granny squares with solid side, cuff, and button bands.

## Sizing and intended fit
Choose a finished chest 8-14 in larger than the wearer's actual chest.

| Size | To fit chest | Finished chest | Length | Approx yarn |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| XS/S | 30-36 in | 44 in | 23 in | 1,650 yd |
| M/L | 38-44 in | 52 in | 25 in | 2,000 yd |
| XL/2X | 46-52 in | 60 in | 27 in | 2,350 yd |
| 3X/4X | 54-60 in | 68 in | 29 in | 2,750 yd |
| 5X | 62-66 in | 74 in | 30 in | 3,100 yd |

## Materials
- DK-weight washable wool or wool blend
- MC cream: approximately 55 percent of total
- Five contrast colors: divide remaining yardage
- 4 mm hook for motifs
- 3.5 mm hook for bands
- 5-7 buttons, 0.75 in
- Tapestry needle and markers

## Gauge
One blocked 4-round square = 4 in.
18 dc and 9 rows = 4 in in solid double crochet.

## Square counts
Each size uses 4 in squares.

| Size | Back grid | Each front | Each sleeve | Total squares |
|---|---|---|---|---:|
| XS/S | 5 x 5 | 2 x 5 | 3 x 4 | 59 |
| M/L | 6 x 6 | 3 x 6 | 3 x 5 | 78 |
| XL/2X | 7 x 7 | 3 x 7 plus side band | 4 x 5 | 111 |
| 3X/4X | 8 x 7 plus hem band | 4 x 7 | 4 x 6 | 132 |
| 5X | 9 x 7 plus side bands | 4 x 7 plus front bands | 5 x 6 | 151 |

The solid crochet bands listed below refine final width and length. Lay out motifs before joining.

## Floral square

### Round 1
With flower-center color, MR, ch 1, 12 sc in ring. Join. Fasten off.

### Round 2
Join petal color. In each stitch around work (sl st, ch 2, 2 dc, ch 2, sl st). Fasten off.

### Round 3
Join leaf color behind petals. Work 12 sc separated by ch 2 around the rear posts. Join.

### Round 4
Sl st to first space. *(3 dc, ch 2, 3 dc) in corner space, 3 dc in each of next two spaces; repeat from * around. Join.

### Round 5
Join MC. Work standard granny-square round: (3 dc, ch 2, 3 dc) in each corner and 3 dc in every side space. Block to 4 in.

Note: The exact number of rounds needed depends on gauge. Stop when the square is 4 in after blocking.

## Layout and joining
Create balanced but non-repeating color placement. Use a continuous flat slip-stitch join in MC, or mattress seam for a less visible join.

1. Assemble back grid.
2. Assemble each front grid.
3. Assemble sleeves as rectangles.
4. Block panels to measurements before seaming.

## Shoulder seams
Place fronts on back, RS together. Seam outer shoulder edges, leaving the center neck opening:
- XS/S: 8 in
- M/L: 9 in
- XL/2X: 10 in
- 3X/4X: 11 in
- 5X: 12 in

## Sleeve placement
Mark center of each sleeve top. Align with shoulder seam. Seam sleeve top evenly to body edge. The armhole depth should be:
- XS/S: 8 in
- M/L: 9 in
- XL/2X: 10 in
- 3X/4X: 11 in
- 5X: 12 in

## Side and sleeve seams
With RS together, seam from cuff to underarm and down body side. Try on before fastening off. Add or remove a narrow solid-dc side strip if more adjustment is needed.

## Hem extension
Join MC at lower front edge. Work 3-7 rows dc evenly across the entire lower edge until target length is reached. Turn each row.

## Cuffs
With smaller hook, join MC at sleeve seam.
Round 1: Sc evenly around, reducing to a comfortable wrist circumference.
Rounds 2-8: Ch 1, sc BLO around. Join.
Fasten off.

## Front and neck band
With smaller hook and RS facing, join MC at lower right front.

Row 1: Sc evenly up right front, around back neck, and down left front. Use approximately 3 sc per two dc-row ends. Turn.
Rows 2-7: Ch 1, sc BLO across. Turn.

### Buttonholes
On Row 4, position 5-7 buttonholes evenly on the wearer's right front. At each marker: ch 2, skip 2 stitches. On next row, work 2 sc in each ch-2 space.

Fasten off after Row 7. Sew buttons opposite holes.

## Optional pockets - make 2
Ch 25.
Row 1: Dc in fourth ch and across. 22 dc.
Rows 2-9: Ch 3, dc across.
Edging: Sc around three outer sides. Block. Sew to fronts 2-3 in above hem and 2 in from band.

## Finishing
Weave ends securely. Soak 15 minutes, squeeze in towel, shape to schematic measurements, and dry flat. Do not hang while wet.

## Fit adjustments
- Add vertical side bands for width without making additional motifs.
- Add rows at hem for length.
- Shorten sleeves by removing motif rows or making the cuff deeper.
- For a less oversized fit, choose one size down only after comparing finished chest.

## Troubleshooting
**Panels do not match:** Reblock all motifs using the same template.
**Heavy garment:** Choose a light DK wool rather than cotton.
**Front edge waves:** Pick up fewer band stitches.
**Front edge pulls:** Pick up more band stitches.
