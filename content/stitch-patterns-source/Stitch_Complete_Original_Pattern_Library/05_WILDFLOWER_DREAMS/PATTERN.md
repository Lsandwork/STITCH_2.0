# Wildflower Dreams Embroidery

A balanced wildflower bouquet designed for a 6 in hoop.

## Materials
- 9 x 9 in piece of linen-cotton fabric
- 6 in embroidery hoop
- Embroidery needle sizes 5-9
- Six-strand cotton floss:
  - dark green
  - sage green
  - coral
  - rose
  - golden yellow
  - sky blue
  - deep blue
  - lavender
  - cream
  - brown
- Transfer pen or washable stabilizer
- Small sharp scissors
- Optional felt backing

## Preparation
1. Wash, dry, and press fabric.
2. Transfer the bouquet with the lowest stem ending 0.75 in above the hoop bottom.
3. Mount fabric drum-tight but not distorted.
4. Separate floss strands before recombining.

## Color and stitch key
- Main stems: dark green, 2 strands, stem stitch
- Fine branches: sage, 1 strand, backstitch
- Leaves: sage/dark green, 2 strands, fishbone stitch
- Pink daisy petals: coral/rose, 3 strands, lazy daisy
- Yellow centers: golden yellow, 3 strands, French knots
- Blue spike flowers: blue shades, 2 strands, detached chain and straight stitch
- Lavender filler: lavender, 2 strands, French knots
- Cream seed heads: cream, 1 strand, straight stitch
- Brown calyx details: brown, 1 strand, backstitch

## Stitching order

### 1. Main stems
Begin with the longest central stem. Use 2 strands dark green and stem stitch. Keep stitch length approximately 1/8 in. Add all primary stems from bottom upward.

### 2. Secondary branches
Use 1 strand sage and backstitch. Branches should taper visually by shortening stitches near the tip.

### 3. Leaves
Work each leaf with fishbone stitch:
1. Make one straight center stitch from base to tip.
2. Alternate diagonal stitches from outer edge across the center line.
3. Keep stitches close but avoid layering a thick ridge.
Use dark green for lower leaves and sage for upper leaves.

### 4. Large coral flower
Outline five petals with split stitch using 2 strands coral. Fill with long-and-short stitch, blending coral into rose near the petal bases. Leave a small open center. Fill center with 12-18 golden French knots, wrapping needle twice.

### 5. Pink daisies
For each daisy, work 8-12 lazy-daisy petals using 3 strands. Anchor each chain with a tiny straight stitch. Fill center with French knots.

### 6. Blue bell spike
Stem stitch the central stalk. Work small detached-chain bells alternating left and right. Add a one-stitch dark-blue shadow at the base of each bell.

### 7. Small blue star flowers
Work five straight stitches radiating from a center point. Add one cream French knot in the center.

### 8. Lavender filler
Scatter groups of three French knots at branch tips. Use one-wrap knots for smaller buds and two-wrap knots for open blooms.

### 9. Seed heads
Create a fine cream stem with backstitch. Add 5-7 radiating straight stitches. Place a tiny brown straight stitch at each tip.

### 10. Grounding details
Add two or three short curved grass blades near the bouquet base using 1 strand dark green and stem stitch.

## Finishing
1. Remove transfer marks according to pen instructions.
2. Let fabric dry completely.
3. Re-hoop with grain straight.
4. Trim fabric to a 1.5 in margin.
5. Run gathering stitches around the excess and pull toward center.
6. Cover back with a 5.75 in felt circle using whip stitch.

## Scaling
For an 8 in hoop, print or trace the design at 130 percent and use slightly longer stitches. Do not increase strand count automatically; test first.

## Troubleshooting
**Puckering:** Reduce thread tension and avoid pulling satin stitches tight.
**Uneven French knots:** Keep working thread taut until needle is almost through fabric.
**Transfer lines show:** Cover with split stitch or remove before final framing.
