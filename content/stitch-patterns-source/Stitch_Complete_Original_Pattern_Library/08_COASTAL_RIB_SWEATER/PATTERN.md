# Coastal Rib Sweater

A relaxed drop-shoulder sweater with broken rib texture, wide sleeves, and clean crew neck.

## Sizing
Designed with 8-12 in positive ease.

| Size | To fit bust | Finished bust | Length | Yarn |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| XS | 30-32 | 40 | 21 | 1,150 yd |
| S | 34-36 | 44 | 22 | 1,300 yd |
| M | 38-40 | 48 | 23 | 1,450 yd |
| L | 42-44 | 52 | 24 | 1,600 yd |
| XL | 46-48 | 56 | 25 | 1,800 yd |
| 2X | 50-52 | 60 | 26 | 2,000 yd |
| 3X | 54-56 | 64 | 27 | 2,200 yd |
| 4X | 58-60 | 68 | 28 | 2,400 yd |
| 5X | 62-64 | 72 | 29 | 2,650 yd |

## Materials
Aran-weight wool or wool blend.
5 mm and 5.5 mm needles.
Stitch holders, markers, needle.

## Gauge
16 sts and 22 rows = 4 in in broken rib after blocking.

## Broken rib
Row 1 RS: Knit.
Row 2 WS: *K1, p1; repeat.
Repeat these two rows.

## Back
With smaller needles CO:
80 (88, 96, 104, 112, 120, 128, 136, 144) sts.

Work k2, p2 rib for 2.5 in, increasing or decreasing 0-2 stitches on final row if needed to preserve texture.

Switch to larger needles. Work broken rib until piece measures:
20 (21, 22, 23, 24, 25, 26, 27, 28) in from cast-on.

Shoulders:
BO 24 (27, 30, 33, 36, 39, 42, 45, 48) sts, place center 32 (34, 36, 38, 40, 42, 44, 46, 48) sts on holder, BO remaining shoulder.

## Front
Work as back until 3 in shorter than finished back.

Neck:
Work 30 (33, 36, 39, 42, 45, 48, 51, 54) sts, join second ball and BO center 20 (22, 24, 26, 28, 30, 32, 34, 36) sts, work remaining side.

Shape each neck edge every RS row:
BO 3 sts once, 2 sts once, then decrease 1 st twice. Continue until front matches back. BO shoulders.

## Sleeves - make 2
With smaller needles CO 36 (36, 40, 40, 44, 44, 48, 48, 52) sts.
Work k2, p2 rib for 3 in.
Switch to larger needles and broken rib.

Increase one stitch at each edge every:
8th (8th, 8th, 8th, 6th, 6th, 6th, 6th, 6th) row
until 60 (62, 66, 68, 72, 76, 80, 84, 88) sts.

Work even until sleeve measures 17 (17, 17.5, 17.5, 18, 18, 18.5, 18.5, 19) in. BO loosely.

## Assembly
Block pieces before seaming.
Sew shoulder seams.
Mark armhole depth on body:
7.5 (8, 8.5, 9, 9.5, 10, 10.5, 11, 11.5) in down from shoulder.
Center sleeve top at shoulder seam and mattress stitch into opening.
Sew sleeve and side seams.

## Neckband
With smaller circular needle, starting at right shoulder, pick up and knit approximately:
96 (100, 104, 108, 112, 116, 120, 124, 128) sts around neck.
Count must be divisible by 4.
Work k2, p2 rib for 1.25 in. BO in pattern using a stretchy bind-off.

## Finishing
Wet block gently to schematic dimensions. Avoid hanging.

## Adjustments
- Add body length before shoulder shaping.
- For a narrower sleeve, stop increases 4-8 stitches earlier.
- For split hem, work front and back separately for first 4 in before joining side seam.
- Substitute cotton only after testing growth; cotton produces a heavier garment.

## Troubleshooting
**Rib expands too much:** Use a smaller needle or wool with more recovery.
**Neckband stands up:** Pick up fewer stitches.
**Shoulder drops too low:** Choose a size with less positive ease.
